Methodology

A CVE can be confirmed as exploited in the wild without knowing who used it. A CVE can also be associated with ransomware activity without knowing which ransomware group used it.

Separately, a CVE can be confirmed or credibly reported as used by a named actor or group, such as APT29, MuddyWater, Dragonfly, UNC3886, or another tracked threat group.

These are all valid intelligence states, but they should not be treated as the same thing. Actor-to-CVE attribution is uncommon, but valuable when confirmed or credibly reported by reliable public sources. This page tracks only source-backed actor-to-CVE relationships.

Confidence Legend

Confirmed

The source explicitly says the named actor exploited, used, or leveraged the CVE.

Reported

Credible public reporting associates the actor with the CVE, but the wording may be less direct or attribution may be based on reporting context.

Suspected

Plausible but uncertain. Suspected rows are visually de-emphasized and hidden by default in the table filters.

Unattributed

Exploitation activity exists, but no named actor is confirmed. This should usually be explanatory or contextual rather than mixed into the actor table.

Disclaimer
